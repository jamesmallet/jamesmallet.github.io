NERC Post-doctoral position: Phylogeography

A three-year Post-doctoral Research Fellow is required to investigate gene genealogies and phylogeography of Amazonian butterflies (Heliconiinae and Ithomiinae).  The work will employ a novel, reductionist approach to the biogeography of a “suture zone” in Peru to test models of the evolution of Amazonian biodiversity.

The successful applicant will have experience and interest in mtDNA and nuclear gene technology and data analysis to test evolutionary hypotheses. It is also hoped to employ a PhD student starting at the same time to work on related aspects of this project, and there will be support available from a technician, Fraser Simpson. Salary will start at points 6-8, (£22,445 - £24,325, including London Weighting, pay award pending), depending on experience, funded by a 3-year NERC research grant (see the full grant proposal for details).

  

Heliconiine, ithomiine and pericopine Lepidoptera belonging to the "melanic tiger" mimicry ring from near Tarapoto, NE Peru.

To apply, send CV, names, addresses, phone numbers and email addresses of two referees, and a covering letter explaining how you could contribute to the project, to James Mallet, Galton Laboratory, UCL.  For full contact and research group details, see our home page or phone James Mallet at (+44)-(0)20-7679-7412.

Closing date: Thursday 15 April 2004, or until a suitable candidate is found.

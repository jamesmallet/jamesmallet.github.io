Volunteer for Panama fieldwork!

My collaborator, Dr. Chris Jiggins, and my PhD student, Vanessa Bull, require suitable undergraduate volunteers who are enthusiastic about  fieldwork to go out to Panama this summer (July-September 2001) and help with experiments on mimicry.  This will involve studying the behaviour of caged birds when confronted with warningly coloured butterflies, and other experiments on mimicry.  The project may involve mist-netting, feeding and keeping birds in cages (and releasing them thereafter), as well as rearing butterflies in cages as part of our experimental work in Panama.  In addition, there would be fieldwork involving butterflies and birds in  rainforests of the National Park, near Gamboa, Panama. The position is not well paid; only some costs are available.  However, the project will be a rare experience for tropical biology, and given proper planning, could be used towards an undergraduate project.  Living costs are cheap in Panama.  It would suit a second year student or a final year student who hasn't many commitments this summer yet.

Interested volunteers might also like to browse Chris Jiggins' website or see Vanessa Bull's advertisement or for that matter my own (see below).  If still interested, they should contact me at: j.mallet@ucl.ac.uk
